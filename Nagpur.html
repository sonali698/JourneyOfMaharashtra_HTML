Nagpur

Nagpur is called as the winter capital of Maharashtra and the third largest city too. It is also proposed to be one of the smart cities of the country is also called the Tiger Capital. What makes the city really popular in India is its extensive production of oranges, which also earned Nagpur a title "Orange city" as well. Dotted with several historical and religious sites and a few modern day attractions, Nagpur makes a nice place to spend a holiday in Maharashtra.

How To Reach Nagpur?

Nagpur is a famous city of India. It is also the commercial hub of Maharashtra. A proper roadway connects the city with various other parts of the country. The nearest places to Nagpur which are important for commerce as well as residence are Pune and Mumbai. Well built roads and railways connect the city throughout.

BY AIR:

Nagpur is on important places of the country. Hence it has an international terminus too. The airport is just 10km away from the main city and has a perfect flow of vehicles to it. Carriers such as Air India, Indigo, Jet airways all have frequent flights in them.

BY RAIL:

Nagpur Junction is an important rail terminus of the country. It is the main junction between the central and the south eastern railway. A number of trains run between Mumbai, Pune, Hyderabad, Kolkata, Lucknow, Chennai etc.

BY ROAD:

Being a big and essential city, Nagpur is connected by a well maintained highway. The NH-6 or the Hajira- Kolkata highway connects Nagpur to various parts of the country. There are also two important bus terminus . The Nagpur Bus Sthanak and the Mor Bhawan. Buses from Nasik, Amravati, Hyderabad Mumbai and Pune fly very often.
